# European Heatwave: Worst Ever and Impossible Without Climate Crisis

**Source:** The Guardian, June 26, 2026  
**Topic:** Scientific analysis by World Weather Attribution (WWA) on the record-breaking western European heatwave

---

## Overview

- The current heatwave in western Europe is the **most severe and widespread ever recorded**.
- Scientists state it **would not be possible without human-caused climate change** driven by fossil fuel burning.
- Almost **half of Europe's 850 largest cities** are experiencing their worst-ever heat stress (combination of temperature and humidity).

---

## Key Findings from WWA Analysis

- The heatwave is **at least 2°C hotter** than an equivalent event in 2003, and **3.5°C hotter** than in 1976, due to increased global heating.
- **Night-time temperatures** that harm sleep are **~100 times more likely today than in 2003**.
- **45% of cities over 50,000 people** are enduring their worst wet bulb globe temperatures (WBGT), indicating extreme health risk because high humidity reduces the body's ability to cool itself.
- The current heatwave would not have been possible in June without climate change.
- Natural variability (e.g., El Niño, weather patterns) was **ruled out** as the cause; the primary factor is global heating supercharging the heat.

---

## Record-Breaking Temperatures & Immediate Impacts

- **UK:** Hottest June day ever recorded – **36.7°C (98.06°F)** in Somerset.
- **Met Office** extended red heat-health alert to 11pm Friday.
- **London Ambulance Service** responded to 641 life-threatening emergencies on Wednesday – its **highest single-day total ever**.
- Across Europe: 
  - Sharp rise in medical emergencies and deaths (exact toll pending statistical analysis).
  - Schools closed, hospitals struggling (machines/IT failing), rail and air travel cancelled.
  - At least **100 million people** expected to face temperatures above 35°C on Thursday.
- **Capital cities** saw their hottest 3-day period ever, even outside of June.

## Critical Quotes

> **Dr Theodore Keeping, Imperial College London (WWA team member):**  
> "This is the most severe and widespread heatwave to have ever affected this large a region of Europe. … This event would not have been possible in June without climate change. But do we expect this to be a cool summer going forward? That's absolutely the case."

> **Simon Stiell, UN Climate Chief:**  
> "Climate change is running rampant, caused by the world's addiction to burning coal, oil and gas. But the solutions are equally clear: a faster shift to clean energy – which is now much cheaper than fossil fuels – as well as protecting forests and building climate resilience."

> **Prof Friederike Otto, Imperial College London (WWA co-founder):**  
> "Scientists like me are beginning to sound like a broken record, reacting year after year to heat extremes that climb ever higher. Yes this is climate change, yes it's us, yes we have the solutions, no we're not implementing them fast enough. It's really now a question of what kind of future we want for ourselves, and whether we're willing to do what it takes to secure it."

> **Carolina Pereira Marghidan, Red Cross Red Crescent Climate Centre:**  
> "After the devastating 2003 heatwave in Europe, many countries invested in early warning systems and action plans. Research shows that those have saved many lives, but it's not enough. … We need greater investment in heat-resilient homes, cities and infrastructure to keep people safe."

## Wider Context & Policy Responses

- **2022 heatwave:** Over 60,000 heat-related deaths in Europe.
- **2024 study:** In just 12 cities during a smaller heatwave, >2,300 people died in 3 days; two-thirds of those deaths were attributed to climate change.
- **UK specific:**
  - Climate Change Committee warned (May 2026) that UK infrastructure is "built for a climate that no longer exists."
  - Over 10,000 summer heatwave deaths in England and Wales from 2020–2024 (UKHSA).
- **Global scale:** Last October, health experts reported that rising heat is now killing **one person per minute** worldwide.
- **Solutions highlighted:** Rapid clean energy transition, forest protection, climate resilience investments, improved early warning systems.
