## Politics & Government

### Karina Milei: The Real Power Behind the Throne
- **Interview with National Deputy Marcela Pagano** (by Jorge Fontevecchia)
  - Pagano claims President Javier Milei's government **"has covered up Manuel Adorni's corruption"** and that voters have fallen out of love with La Libertad Avanza.
  - **Key quote:**  
    > "It's become evident that it is Karina Milei who calls the shots."

### Manuel Adorni Resignation & Fallout
- **Adorni resigned as Cabinet Chief** amid a corruption investigation.
  - Accused the press of **"media attacks"** in his resignation statement.
  - Admitted he concealed **US$500,000** from asset declarations.
  - Investigations focus on a private jet trip to Uruguay, real estate investments, and questionable purchases (including a video game console on staffers' credit cards).
- **Polling:** Argentines **overwhelmingly disapprove** of Milei's handling of the scandal.
- **Analysis by Agustino Fontevecchia**: Called Adorni's departure **"pretty pathetic"** – he built his tenure on arrogance and now stands accused of corruption.
- **Timeline:** Resignation came after months of pressure; Milei initially brushed off revelations as opposition/media attacks.

### Cabinet Reshuffle: Diego Santilli Steps In
- **Santilli sworn in as new Cabinet Chief**, replacing Adorni.
- Santilli is a **lifetime politician** from PRO, once derided by Milei as part of "the caste."
- Milei's **fourth Cabinet chief in 30 months**, a turnover rate that a university study says has gutted the post's constitutional power.
- **Analysis:** The corruption scandals are forcing Milei to turn to the very establishment he vilified.

### Fundación Faro – Opaque Finances
- Libertarian think tank linked to Milei insiders saw a **400‑fold surge in donations**, reaching **US$4.8 million**.
- Regulators and judges demand the foundation **name its donors** amid concerns over opaque finances.

### Other Political Developments
- **Kicillof admits:** Alberto Fernández's Peronist government **"didn't work"** and its breakdown led to Milei's election. (Video call with Peronist leaders)
- **BlackRock warning:** Argentina's bond rally will hit a wall because of **past defaults and next year's presidential elections**.

## Economy

### Mixed Indicators
- **Economic activity fell 1.5% in April** month‑on‑month, but grew 1.6% year‑on‑year.
- **Q1 2026 GDP rose 2.3%** year‑on‑year, driven by exports and extractive industries.
- Contrast: **Exports boom, but households struggle** – unemployment up, bank defaults hit a 20‑year high, manufacturing contracting.
- **Buenos Aires City poverty rate rises** after five quarters of decline: **over 651,000 porteños** (more than 1 in 5) in poverty in Q1 2026.

### Debt & Finance
- **Central Bank extends repo maturities** worth **US$6 billion** past the election to ease government debt burden.
- **Genneia SA files for IPO in the US** – the first Argentine company to do so since 2019.

### Key Sectors
- **Beef exports to the US** boom, but locals pay more for steak as exports shift from premium to lower‑quality cuts.
- **Milei tries again to revive Argentina's dormant mortgage market** after earlier surge in mortgage‑backed home sales evaporated due to interest rates above 100%.
- **Vaca Muerta overtakes agriculture** and is expected to finance Argentina's future (column by Carlos Burgueño).
